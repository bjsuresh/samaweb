Case Study- Optimal Load Distribution & scheduling For thermal generating units, the cost of fuel per unit power output varies significantly with the power output of the unit. Therefore one needs to consider the fuel cost characteristics of the generators while finding their optimal real power outputs. For economic generation scheduling to meet a particular load demand, if transmission losses are neglected, all plants must operate at equal incremental production costs, subject to the constraint that the total generation be equal to the demand. As the demand changes, units are loaded in such a way that the incremental fuel cost (IFC) of operating units reaches the same λ, the Lagrange multiplier. This involves calculation of heat rate, incremental fuel cost and distributing the load so that incremental fuel cost remains same among all generating units. Typical payback period is less than 2 months.
